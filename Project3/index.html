Mass public shootings are a tragic and devastating phenomenon that has occurred with alarming frequency in the USA in recent years. According to the Database, which defines a mass shooting as an incident in which four or more people are shot, there were 185 mass public shooting incidents in the USA from 1966 to 2022. These incidents resulted in a total of 1363 deaths and 2135 injuries.

Mental State of Perpetrators

Mass Shooters by Mental Illness Diagnosis

The mental state of perpetrators can vary widely depending on the individual and the situation. In some cases, perpetrators may suffer from mental illnesses or personality disorders that contribute to their actions.

In other cases, they may be motivated by factors such as anger, revenge, jealousy, or a desire for power and control. Some common mental health conditions that may be present in perpetrators include antisocial personality disorder, narcissistic personality disorder, borderline personality disorder, and psychosis. These conditions may contribute to violent or criminal behavior in some individuals.

Analyzing the Trends: Population Growth and Mass Shooting Yearly Growth Rates in the United States

The United States is the third most populous country in the world, with an estimated population of 332 million people in 2021. The population of the United States has been steadily increasing over the years, but the growth rate has been declining. In the last decade, the population growth rate was around 0.7% per year, but in 2020 it was only 0.35%.

There is no clear correlation between the growth rate of the population and the occurrence of mass shootings in the United States. While the population of the United States has been increasing at a declining rate, the number of mass shootings has been increasing in recent years. It's important to note that mass shootings are a complex issue influenced by various factors, including gun laws, mental health, social and economic factors, and other societal issues. Additionally, population growth can also be influenced by various factors such as immigration, birth rates, and mortality rates. Therefore, it is not appropriate to draw a direct correlation between the two factors.
